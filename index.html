  • 1986

    Am I the sum of my parts? Am I just a programmer? I wanted to write code since the day I realised what a computer was capable of doing... I have never looked back. I might go so far as to say that I was born with code in my veins...

  • 2001

    I started in 2001, writing Java code - with no internet access, I had to "unzip" the jar files to make sense of everything... it was an arduous process, and I spent sleepless nights trying to digest the information ...

    At the time I had no formal background in coding, when I started I didn't even own a computer - relying on printouts my friends so that I could study the code and make the best of my time I could borrow with the school computers.

    Having quickly excelled, I mastered Java within a few months, going from Turtle, to AWT, to Applets I grew tired of the language and the limitations it imposed on developers, such as the SandBox model.

  • 2002-2003

    That's when I met C ... my life changed in ways I never thought possible, by 2002 I was a competent C programmer, having taught myself the basics using Java, and working hard to grasp the inner workings of the computer from studying what little code I could convince my friends to print for me... I managed to secure a computer and spent all the free time I could muster staring at the screen, making things happen, it was wonderful.

  • 2004-2007

    My journey through life has always kept code on my path, earning a bursary to the University of Stellenbosch, I managed to find a brand new course that allowed me to study code without restrictions - the Uni had internet access, and it was here that my thirst for hunger would be quenched ... or so I thought...

    At first all was good, I quickly found myself doing programming odd jobs for friends on campus, a hobby that would earn me my first programming job - developer for a Student Society, the US Otaku.

    During that time, I worked tirelessly to create an online presence for our team, and create interactive games and demos which would help to spread our message, things were going well and I was nominated as Vice Chair to the society ... Even as V.C I clung to my programming role, rarely seen at meetings, but always online... this was the life I had dreamed of...

  • 2007-2013

    After 3.5 years of excelling at code, and code-related subjects, and sucking at other subjects, like Economics, Psychology, Philosophy ... I learned that I would forever be a code - nothing more, nothing less. This suited me fine, and allowed me to focus on one thing and one thing alone.

    I quickly packed up my things and headed out into the world in search of my coding path in life. Life handed me a telecommute job as an understudy for one of the worlds greatest PHP developers, I sucked at PHP at the time, but I held my ground with all the tasks assigned to me, but it didn't feel right to me - working online, using web technologies...

    I quickly landed a job with a Systems Integration firm who built custom BMS and SCADA systems using a mix of C/C++/Java/SQL/Python etc... I had arrived. Within the space of 5 years, I had managed to work my way up within the company, I was the Senior Programmer, and life was good.

  • 2013

    Until that fateful day when a disagreement with company policy would send my life down a different path... I resigned my job, a few weeks before I was to be married. The wedding went smoothly, and I was doing web development to make ends meet. Life was still good, but it could be better.

    I cast my net and one small company, a startup in the digital publishing arena asked me to help them become grounded in the new era of technological advances. It felt strange to me, being so far from my life's great love, but I accepted the challenge and have never looked back. Quickly being cast as a Digital Book Publisher, and the lone developer for the company, my life headed back in the direction of code and compiled languages. It was a challenge, but one I was happy to undertake. The story continues, let's see how it unfolds...

    I started in 2001, writing Java code - with no internet access, I remember thinking about games as abstract masterpieces ... all I had seen at the time were classics like Donkey Kong, MS Pacman and Mario Bros. I longed to create my own games.

    Drawing inspiration from the familiar, I spent hours creating little Applets which would each serve to strengthen my skills, until I had created dozens of little games - each better than the last.

eBook Designer...

  • 2013

  • 2013

    Am I just a eBook Designer?

    I have been working with digital books since April of this year, it's an interesting field and the company for which I work is at the bleeding edge of digital book technology.

    I find myself with the good fortune of being able to convert an EPUB2 into an EPUB3 by hand, with all the knowledge I've gained oer the past couple of months my company is poised to take the digital publishing market by storm.

Parkour Traceur...

  • 2004 - 2013

  • 2004 - 2013

    Am I just a Parkour Traceur?

    I remember the first true Parkour video I watched. A YouTube video of David Belle performing an insane stunt of some kind, the details escape me now, but the emotions will live with me forever...

    I immediately set out to copy the moves as best I could, having only YouTube videos to work from - and not knowing the name of the sport, I fared clumsily. I packed on injury after injury until I met a friend who helped me do the proper research to refine my technique.

    Not too long after that I joined up with other traceurs, people in and around Cape Town who were trying to improve their skills. We learned a lot from each other, and regular meetings still happen to this day...

Biker...

  • 2007
  • 2010
  • 2012

  • 2007

    Am I just a Biker?

    I remember the first motorcycle I ever owned, a 125cc Moto Mia - Mocca. A good friend of mine allowed me to purchase the bike from him on terms... with my meagre salary at the time it was all I could afford.

    I had wanted to be a "biker" for as long as I could remember, I was an avid cyclist for years - preparing myself for the joy of powered locomotion on two wheels.

    I still remember the first time I got onto a motorcycle, my friend had just handed over the keys to the bike, and was teaching me how to drive in a vacant parking lot... after 5 minutes I knew I would be doing this for a long time. The net day, without experience and without a license, I took the bike to work - in Bellville, over 20 kilometers from home... exciting times...

  • 2010

    After several years of "cutting my teeth" on the roads, I bit the bullet and acquired my learner's license. At around the same time I decided to "upgrade" and purchased a 200cc Loncin Lx-200 2 motorcycle. What a beauty, nippy engine, easy on the turns, just a joy to drive.

    I remember going on long drives with that bike, she loved the open road as much as I did...

    I had my fair share of mishaps, my chain broke a couple of times - far away from home... leaving me to push the bike as far as possible towards a garage or landmark, then calling for help... Or reaching the bike to discover that she didn't want to start for lack of petrol ... lot's of pushing to get her some juice... good times...

  • 2012

    I decided to upgrade again, this time I got my hands on a superbike - Suzuki RF600R, a 600cc beast which had way more power than I knew how to handle...

    I realised too late that I would struggle to get spare parts for her, but I loved my "Suzie", I hunted and drove and called around when I needed parts, eventually I would find the FR owners club - an online community of bikers with the same bike... I wish I had found them sooner.

    These days my wife and I travel the open roads, with a super bike taking us wherever we want to go. There were some difficulties, but we are hitting our stride, and life is good. We have travelled to some crazy places - Langebaan, Piketberg, Paarl, Kommetjie, Partridge Point, the Cable Cars and we are planning a drive down to Hermanus soon - see you there.
